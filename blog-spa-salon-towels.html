Day spa & salon towels: an Australian buyer's guide

Published 7 June 2026 · 7 min read · By the ALT Trade Desk · Griffith, NSW

Towels in a salon or day spa do more work than almost any other linen in hospitality. They're soaked, bleached, dyed on, wrapped around heads, and laundered daily — then expected to still look professional in front of a paying client. Buy the wrong ones and you're replacing stained, thinning towels every few months. Buy the right ones and they outlast the trend cycle.

Here's what actually matters when stocking a day spa, salon, or wellness clinic in Australia.

Quick Answer For most salons, 400–500 GSM is the sweet spot — quick-drying and durable for high turnover. Step up to 500–600 GSM for spa treatment rooms where the towel is part of the experience. Choose black, bleach-proof towels for any station that touches hair colour or chemicals.

Why black bleach-proof towels are the salon standard

Black is the most popular salon towel colour for one practical reason: it hides bleach spots, hair dye, and chemical discolouration better than any other shade.1 A white towel near a colour station is stained within a week. But not every black towel is equal — the ones worth buying are specifically bleach-proof (also called bleach-resistant or bleach-safe), manufactured with dyeing processes that lock colour into the fibre so peroxide, developer, and toner don't fade them.1

The payoff is longevity: a true bleach-proof salon towel holds its colour and softness through 100+ bleach cycles, where an ordinary dyed towel goes patchy and grey in a fraction of that.1 For a colour-heavy salon, this single specification is the difference between replacing towels quarterly and replacing them yearly. The right GSM for salon vs spa

It's tempting to assume thicker is better. In high-turnover salons, it isn't — a 400–500 GSM towel actually performs better than a heavy one because it dries faster between clients and survives more daily wash cycles.1 Save the plush weights for the treatment room, where the towel is part of how the client feels.

The sizes that cover a treatment menu

You don't need a dozen sizes. Three cover almost every service:1

  • Salon hand towel — around 40×70 cm (16×27"): the industry-standard salon towel. The right balance of coverage and manageability for hair, neck, and station use.
  • Face washer — around 33×33 cm (13×13"): for facials, cleansing, and detail work.
  • Bath towel — 70×140 cm and up: for full-service spas offering showers, body treatments, or wraps.

Stock the hand towel deepest — it's the workhorse — and the larger sizes to match the treatments you actually offer.

How many towels do you actually need?

A reliable rule of thumb for salons is 8–12 towels per styling chair, per day.1 Then you need enough in rotation to cover towels in use, towels in the wash, and a reserve — the same 3-PAR thinking that runs the rest of hospitality.

Worked example A 6-chair salon, open 6 days a week, churns through roughly 50–75 towels a day. Add a backup set in the laundry cycle and you want 100–150 towels in total rotation to never run short mid-service.1

Day spas calculate differently — base it on treatment rooms and average treatments per day rather than chairs — but the principle holds: count peak daily usage, then multiply for wash and reserve.

Laundering that protects the investment

Bleach-proof towels are built to take chlorine, but technique still matters. Industry care guidance suggests up to roughly half a cup of chlorine bleach per load on bleach-safe towels without compromising the fabric, and a hot wash effectively lifts hair dye, peroxide, and toner.1 Wash colour-contaminated towels separately, avoid overloading the machine, and don't over-dry — excess heat is what hardens and shortens the life of any commercial towel.
